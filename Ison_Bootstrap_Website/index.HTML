Discover the fascinating world of capybaras, also known as "Coconut Doggies" - the world's largest rodents! These remarkable creatures are native to South America and are found in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, forests, and near bodies of water.

About Capybaras

Capybaras are large, herbivorous, semiaquatic rodents found in South America. They are well adapted to a semi-aquatic lifestyle and are commonly found near water bodies such as rivers, lakes, and ponds.

They are known for their social behavior and often live in groups of 10 to 20 individuals. Capybaras are gentle creatures and are often seen interacting peacefully with other animals.
